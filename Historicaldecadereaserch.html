Historical Decade Reaserch

A diagram of an MSAW (inaugural minimum safe altitude warning system) this system calculates the planes location, and compares it to the data available from land, and calculates the trajectory of the plane while landing and giving the pilots warnings if their trajectory is too low, or too high.

• On November 5, 1976, the FAA installed the "inaugural minimum safe altitude warning system" in Los Angeles International Airport, with the ability of monitoring aircraft altitudes and calculating dangerous situations by comparing them to available terrain maps.


• In October 28, 1977, the MSAW (Minimum safe altitude warning) system was in use at all 63 of the main U.S. airports that had ARTS III(Automated Radar Terminal System III) radar systems.


• In August 4, 1976 the FAA made the mandatory age retirement for pilots be 65.


• By November 20, 1976, canine squads were deployed to 29 U.S. airports to make sure each airplane in the U.S. airspace was in a 30-minute reach of an explosives detection dog.


• Meteorologists from the National Weather Service began working at 13 FAA air route traffic control centers on April 17, 1976, creating a new agreement between the two organizations.


• President Carter authorized a "Airline Deregulation Act" of 1978 on October 24, allowing reductions of ticket prices up to 70% without approval and the addition of new airlines on routes not covered by regulations.


• On December 27, 1976, the FAA created a program that aimed at decreasing midair collisions by 80%.
