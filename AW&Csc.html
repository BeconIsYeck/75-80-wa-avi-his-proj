Involved Science in AEW&C Radars

• AEW&C radars are based off of AESA (Actively Electronically Scanned Array) radars, which split one radar into many different smaller T/R modules to emit multiple pulses at once.


• These T/R Modules, when combined, can track multiple targets in the air at once and can even turn electronically, making operating the radar much easier for the crew.


• All of the T/R Modules comprise the entire radar, which is covered by a rotodome, which a rotating cover than helps with protecting the radar and giving it 360° of coverage.



An AEW&C AESA Radar used in a Chinese military surveillance aircraft circa 2006. AEW&C radars like this are commonly used on military ships and helicopters as well as aircraft.
