Involved Science in Jumbo Jets

• Turbofan Jet Engines are the most common type of engine on large planes like the Boeing 747.


• What made the Turbofan Jet Engines used on the Boeing 747 so special was that they were giagantic compared to other engines at that time.


• Just like all other jet engines, the ones on the 747 are comprised of an inlet, compressor, combustor, turbine, and a nozzle all encased in a metal cover.


• It works by intaking air through the inlet, and then letting some of the air pass into the compressor and into the combustor, then igniting with the fuel creating a small explosion.


• The rest of the air that was intaken then heats up and becomes steam, which powers the turbine as well as expells out of the engine through the nozzle, creating thrust.
