Stealth fighters

• The first stealth-oriented plane ever made was the Lockheed Have Blue, which was a prototype stealth fighter-like plane made by the Lockheed Company in 1977.


• It was made by the Skunk Works part of the company, and the project was headed by American aerospace engineer Kelly Johnson.


• Due to the devestating loss of aircraft endured by the U.S. in the Vietnam War, Lockheed started the "Hopeless Diamond" project in hopes of making a radar-proof plane.



An early Lockheed Have Blue prototype sitting in a warehouse circa 1978. This plane may be either prototype "HB1001" or prototype "HB1002", but since both prototypes have been lost, it is impossible to check.
