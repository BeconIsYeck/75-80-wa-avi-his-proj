Involved Science in Stealth Fighters

• The primary idea behind stealth planes is radar concealment, where an aircraft tries to deflect or absorb radar pulses to prevent the aircraft from being detected.


• The RCS (Radar Cross Section) of an object is, simply put, how many pulses / waves are returned back to the radar that has emitted them after bouncing off of an object.


• One way to lower the RCS of a plane is by its shaping, where creating specific setups of facets and edges deflect pulses off of the plane in a different direction than the radar.


• Another way that the RCS of a plane is decreased is by building the plane out of Radar Obsorbing Materials so that the plane can quickly remove a good amount of radar pulses that it interacts with.
